History

A Legacy of Hope & Commitment

Founded in 1995, Zanzavat Bahuudeshiya Shaikshanik Sanstha was established during a critical era of rural development in Maharashtra. The organization began as a small group of educators and social activists who wanted to solve basic resource deficits for students. Over the years, our initiatives expanded, growing from basic tuition groups to full-scale educational sponsorships, health checkup camps, clothing distribution drives, and community food projects.

Our foundational vision has always been to build a strong foundation, or आधार, for every child. We believe that by providing resources and guidance, we can help children break the cycle of poverty and lead a life of dignity, learning, and productivity.

"We do not build systems; we nurture lives. Real change happens when a child receives a notebook and smiles."
— Ajinkya Bhakre, President
Our Core Principles

What Drives Us Forward

Our mission and vision are anchored in building community resilience, expanding opportunities, and providing support.

Our Mission

To uplift underserved children and rural communities by making quality education accessible, organizing primary health services, distributing essential food resources, and running continuous welfare programs that foster self-reliance.

👁️

Our Vision

To create a society built on positive परिवर्तन, where every child receives the necessary resources to learn, grow, and thrive in a safe, healthy, and supportive community environment.

Our Milestones

A Journey Since 1995

A look at the key moments and growth of our organization over the decades.

1995

Foundation & Resolution

Zanzavat Bahuudeshiya Shaikshanik Sanstha was founded to support students facing educational dropouts.

2005

First Rural Medical Camp

Initiated free checkup services and distributed vital medications to over 100 rural families.

2015

Focus on Student Welfare

Launched structured sponsorship programs for uniforms, stationary, and study guidance centers.

2026

Expanded Child Development

Currently supporting 75+ active children, running multiple camps, with a goal to scale to new areas.
